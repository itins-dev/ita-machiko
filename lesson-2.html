Lesson 2 — Self-Introductions

Meeting people · Building confidence · Beginner

⏱ 30 minutes 🎯 Beginner ✈️ Italy-bound
Lesson objective: By the end, she can introduce herself, ask someone's name and where they're from, and keep a short conversation going without hitting a dead end.
1

Warm-up — Lesson 1 Review

Reconnect with what she already knows
3 min

Quick flashcard drill: you say English, she says Italian — greetings and polite phrases from lesson 1. Then ask in English: "Someone at a café asks your name. What do you say?"

Let her guess. It primes the brain and shows how much she retained.

2

Core Introduction Phrases

What to say about yourself
8 min
Italian English
Mi chiamo…My name is…
Sono di…I'm from… (city)
Sono [nazionalità]I'm [nationality] — e.g. Sono australiana
PiacereNice to meet you
Piacere mioThe pleasure is mine
Have her say her own name and city right away — Mi chiamo [name], sono di [city]. Personal examples stick better than abstract drills.
3

Asking Back — Casual vs. Formal

Turn a monologue into a real exchange
7 min
Italian English Register
Come ti chiami? What's your name? Casual — friends, peers
Come si chiama? What's your name? Formal — strangers, older people
Di dove sei? Where are you from? Casual
Di dov'è? Where are you from? Formal
Keep it simple: in tourist contexts (hotels, restaurants, guided tours) the formal is safer. With younger people or fellow travellers, casual is fine. When in doubt — formal.
Drill — back and forth
You: Come ti chiami?
Student: Mi chiamo [name]. E tu?
You: Mi chiamo [name]. Di dove sei?
Student: Sono di [city]. E tu?
Repeat until the exchange flows naturally without pausing to think.
4

Small Talk Extenders

So the conversation doesn't die after 10 seconds
7 min
Italian English
Sto visitando l'ItaliaI'm visiting Italy
È la prima voltaIt's my first time
È bellissimoIt's beautiful
Mi piace moltoI like it a lot
Non parlo bene l'italianoI don't speak Italian well
Sto imparandoI'm learning
Non parlo bene l'italiano is magic — Italians immediately warm up, slow down, and often switch to helping mode. It disarms the whole interaction.
5

Putting It Together — Mini Role-Play

Full scenario from greeting to small talk
3 min
Scenario She's at a hotel breakfast. Another guest (you) strikes up a conversation. She greets them, introduces herself, asks their name and where they're from, mentions it's her first time in Italy, and says she loves it.
Sample flow
Guest (you): Buongiorno! Mi chiamo Marco. E lei?
Student: Buongiorno! Mi chiamo [name]. Piacere.
Guest: Di dov'è?
Student: Sono di [city]. È la prima volta in Italia — è bellissimo!
Guest: Parla bene l'italiano!
Student: Grazie! Sto imparando. Non parlo bene ancora.

Run it twice. The second pass should be smoother and more natural. Praise the attempt, correct one thing at a time.

6

Wrap-up & Homework

Review and build the daily habit
2 min

Quick runthrough: you say English, she says Italian — all phrases from today.

  • ✍️ Write out her own 3-sentence intro: Mi chiamo… Sono di… È la prima volta in Italia. Say it out loud every day until it's effortless.
  • 🗣️ Practice the back-and-forth with a friend, partner, or even herself in the mirror — both the casual and formal versions.
  • 📱 Continue daily Duolingo or phrasebook review — focus on the new vocab from today.

Teacher Notes

  • Don't get deep into grammar — avoid explaining tu vs. Lei conjugation rules in detail. Just label it "casual" and "formal" and move on.
  • If she's confident, add Quanti anni hai? / Ho [x] anni (How old are you? / I'm [x]) as a bonus — but only if time allows.
  • The dialogue drill in section 3 is the most valuable part of this lesson. Don't rush it.
