Lesson 6 — Restaurants

Getting a table · Ordering · Paying · Beginner

⏱ 30 minutes 🎯 Beginner ✈️ Italy-bound
Lesson objective: By the end, she can get a table, read the basic structure of an Italian menu, order confidently, handle dietary needs, and pay — without defaulting to English.
1

Warm-up — Dining Culture

Set expectations, prevent surprises
3 min

Ask in English: "What do you already know about eating out in Italy?" Let her answer freely. Then fill the gaps — a few things that will genuinely help her:

  • Meals are long and unhurried — il conto (the bill) won't arrive until you ask for it.
  • Lunch (pranzo) is still the main meal in many places. Dinner (cena) is typically after 8pm.
  • Cover charge (il coperto) is common — a small fixed fee per person, not a tip.
2

Getting a Table

From arrival to being seated
6 min
Italian English
Un tavolo per due, per favoreA table for two, please
Ho prenotato a nome…I have a reservation under the name…
Avete un tavolo libero?Do you have a free table?
Possiamo sederci fuori?Can we sit outside?
Possiamo sederci vicino alla finestra?Can we sit by the window?
Il menu, per favoreThe menu, please
Avete un menu in inglese?Do you have a menu in English?
Un tavolo per due is the essential opener — everything else follows. Drill it with different party sizes: per uno, per tre, per quattro.
3

Reading the Menu

How an Italian menu is structured
5 min

Italian menus follow a fixed structure. Knowing it prevents confusion and helps her order confidently without asking for English.

Starter
Antipasto
First course
Primo
Second course
Secondo
Side dish
Contorno
Dessert
Dolce
Coffee
Caffè
Sides (contorni) are ordered separately and don't come automatically with the main — a common surprise for non-Italians. Worth flagging.
4

Ordering Food & Drinks

From the waiter's greeting to the last course
7 min
Italian English
Siete pronti per ordinare?Are you ready to order? (what staff will say)
Sì, pregoYes, go ahead / please
Vorrei…I'd like… (the same frame from lesson 4)
Per me…For me… (very natural when ordering)
Cos'è questo?What is this?
Cosa consiglia?What do you recommend?
Acqua naturale / frizzanteStill / sparkling water
Un bicchiere di vino rosso / biancoA glass of red / white wine
Un caffè, per favoreA coffee, please
Sono allergico/a a…I'm allergic to… (-o masc. / -a fem.)
Senza glutineGluten-free
Sono vegetariano/aI'm vegetarian
Cosa consiglia? (What do you recommend?) is a brilliant phrase — it flatters the waiter, produces a good meal, and feels genuinely Italian. Make sure she uses it.
Mini drill You play the waiter, announcing the specials. She orders a starter, a main, and a drink — using Vorrei or Per me. Run it twice: once ordering for one, once ordering for two.
5

During & After the Meal

Feedback, requests, and paying the bill
4 min
Italian English
È deliziosoIt's delicious
Buonissimo!Really good! / Delicious!
Scusi, ho ordinato…Excuse me, I ordered…
Manca ancora…We're still missing…
Il conto, per favoreThe bill, please
Paghiamo separatamenteWe're paying separately
Posso pagare con carta?Can I pay by card? (from lesson 3)
Tenga il restoKeep the change
Il conto, per favore is the most important phrase in this section — the bill will not come on its own. She must ask. Drill it until it's instinctive.
6

Putting It Together — Full Restaurant Visit

Arrival to paying the bill
3 min
Scenario She arrives at a trattoria, asks for a table for two outside, orders drinks and food, asks the waiter for a recommendation, compliments the food, and then requests the bill.
Sample exchange
Waiter (you): Buonasera!
Student: Buonasera! Un tavolo per due, per favore. Possiamo sederci fuori?
Waiter: Certo, prego. (leads to table) Ecco il menu.
Student: Grazie. Cosa consiglia come primo?
Waiter: Le tagliatelle al ragù sono ottime stasera.
Student: Perfetto. Per me le tagliatelle. E acqua frizzante, per favore.
— After the meal —
Student: Buonissimo! Scusi — il conto, per favore.
Waiter: Eccolo. Sono ventitré euro.
Student: Ecco a lei. Tenga il resto.

Run it twice. On the second pass, she orders something different and handles a missing dish. Keep the pace natural — restaurant Italian is fast.

7

Wrap-up & Homework

Consolidate before the next meal in Italy
2 min

Quick runthrough: you play the waiter from arrival to bill — she handles the whole interaction without prompts. Time it. The confidence boost when it flows is real.

  • 🍽️ Find the menu of a restaurant she plans to visit in Italy and identify the antipasti, primi, secondi and dolci. Knowing the structure before she arrives removes 90% of menu anxiety.
  • ☕ Learn the Italian coffee order she'll actually use: un caffè (espresso), un cappuccino, un caffè macchiato. Italians order by name — not "a small latte".
  • 🗣️ Say Il conto, per favore out loud ten times right now. It sounds trivial — but when the moment comes in a busy trattoria, automaticity is everything.

Teacher Notes

  • Don't teach tipping culture as a rule — it's inconsistent across Italy. If she asks: rounding up or leaving small change is appreciated but never expected.
  • Cosa consiglia? is genuinely worth practising until it feels natural — it's the phrase that most makes a tourist feel like a traveller.
  • The menu structure section (3) can be cut short if time is tight — it's background knowledge, not survival vocab.
  • If she has a specific dietary requirement, spend extra time on the allergy/preference phrases and make sure she can say it clearly and confidently. This one matters for safety, not just comfort.
