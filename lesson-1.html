Lesson 1 — Greetings & Polite Phrases

Survival Italian · Travel-focused · Complete Beginner

⏱ 30 minutes 🎯 True Beginner ✈️ Italy-bound
Lesson objective: By the end, she can greet people appropriately by time of day, be polite in any situation, and escape gracefully when she doesn't understand something.
1

Warm-up

Activate prior knowledge, set context
3 min

Ask in English: "Imagine you walk into a café in Rome at 10am. What do you say?"

Let her guess freely — no pressure. This activates her brain, reveals her starting point, and makes the lesson feel personal and urgent.

2

Greetings by Time of Day

Learn, drill, repeat until automatic
7 min
Italian English When to use
Ciao Hi / Bye Friends, young people, casual
Buongiorno Good morning / Good day Until ~2pm, anyone
Buonasera Good evening After ~5pm, anyone
Buonanotte Good night Only when parting at night
Call out a time of day, she gives the right greeting. Repeat until it's automatic — no thinking required.
3

Polite Essentials

Please, thank you & excuse me — with the right nuance
8 min
Italian English
Per favorePlease
GrazieThank you
PregoYou're welcome
Mi scusiExcuse me (to get attention)
PermessoExcuse me (to pass through)
Sono spiacenteI'm sorry
Key distinction: Mi scusi to get someone's attention. Permesso to physically pass someone — Italians notice the difference.
Role-play cue She's navigating a crowded market, then a restaurant. You set the situation, she responds with the right phrase.
4

Survival Phrases

Emergency exits for when things go off-script
7 min
Italian English
Non capiscoI don't understand
Può ripetere?Can you repeat that?
Parla inglese?Do you speak English?
Non parlo italianoI don't speak Italian
Come si dice…?How do you say…?
Italians appreciate the attempt even if she switches to English afterward. The effort matters more than perfection.
5

Putting It Together — Mini Role-Play

Full scenario combining everything learned
3 min
Scenario She walks into a hotel at 7pm. She greets the receptionist, says she doesn't understand something, and asks if they speak English.

Let her stumble. Correct gently. Run it twice — the second time should feel noticeably smoother.

6

Wrap-up & Homework

Quick review and daily practice habit
2 min

Quick flashcard-style runthrough: you say English, she says Italian.

  • 🗣️ Say Buongiorno or Buonasera out loud every morning and evening this week — to herself, a mirror, anyone. Repetition out loud is the whole game at this stage.
  • 📱 Download Duolingo or use a phrasebook app for daily 5-minute review between lessons.

Teacher Notes

  • Don't introduce writing or spelling drills — waste of time at this stage. Keep energy on speaking and listening.
  • If she finishes early, loop back to the role-play with a new scenario: a café, a shop, asking for directions on the street.
  • Focus on pronunciation, not spelling. She needs to say these phrases, not read them.
