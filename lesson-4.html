Lesson 4 — Shopping

Boutiques · Markets · Asking & Buying · Beginner

⏱ 30 minutes 🎯 Beginner ✈️ Italy-bound
Lesson objective: By the end, she can walk into any Italian shop, ask for what she wants, request a size or colour, try something on, and complete the purchase — all in Italian.
1

Warm-up — Numbers & Money Review

Reconnect with lesson 3
3 min

Quick price-reading drill: write 4–5 prices on paper (€7, €23,50, €64, €12,90) and have her say them in Italian. Then ask: "You walk into a clothes shop in Florence. What would you want to be able to say?"

Her answer sets the agenda and makes the lesson feel personally relevant.

2

Asking for What You Want

The most useful frame in any shop
7 min
Italian English
Vorrei…I'd like… (polite, very natural)
Cerco…I'm looking for…
Avete…?Do you have…?
Mi può mostrare…?Can you show me…?
Sto solo guardandoI'm just browsing
Posso aiutarla?Can I help you? (what staff will say)
Vorrei is the go-to phrase — more polite than voglio (I want) and used constantly. Drill it with different items: Vorrei una borsa, Vorrei questo, Vorrei quello.
Mini drill You play a shop assistant greeting her (Posso aiutarla?). She responds using Vorrei or Cerco with any item she can name. Repeat 3–4 times with different items.
3

Sizes & Trying Things On

Clothes shopping essentials
6 min
Italian English
Posso provarlo/a?Can I try it on? (-o masc. / -a fem.)
Dov'è il camerino?Where is the fitting room?
Che taglia è?What size is it?
Avete una taglia più grande?Do you have a bigger size?
Avete una taglia più piccola?Do you have a smaller size?
Mi sta beneIt fits me well
Non mi sta beneIt doesn't fit me
È troppo grande / piccoloIt's too big / small
Italian sizing: clothing uses European sizes (38, 40, 42…). It's worth her knowing her Italian size before the trip — avoids confusion at the fitting room.
4

Colours

Asking for a different colour or spotting one on a tag
5 min
rosso — red
blu — blue
verde — green
giallo — yellow
arancione — orange

Teach the frame: Avete questo in [colour]? — Do you have this in [colour]? One pattern, infinite use.
5

Putting It Together — Boutique Role-Play

Full shop visit from entry to purchase
7 min
Scenario She enters a small clothes shop in Florence. A staff member greets her. She says she's browsing, then asks about a dress, requests her size, asks to try it on, says it's too big and asks for a smaller one, then asks the price and decides to buy it.
Sample exchange
Staff (you): Buongiorno! Posso aiutarla?
Student: Buongiorno! Sto solo guardando, grazie.
Student: Scusi — vorrei questo vestito. Avete la taglia 42?
Staff: Sì, certo. Eccola.
Student: Posso provarlo?
Staff: Certo, il camerino è là.
Student: È troppo grande. Avete una taglia più piccola?
Staff: Sì, ecco la 40.
Student: Mi sta bene! Quanto costa?
Staff: Sessantacinque euro.
Student: Bene, lo prendo. Posso pagare con carta?

Run it twice. On the second pass, change the item and colour — keep her adapting, not just reciting.

6

Wrap-up & Homework

Review and build confidence before the trip
2 min

Quick runthrough: you say English, she says Italian — mix phrases from all four lessons so far.

  • 🛍️ Next time she's in any shop, mentally rehearse the shopping dialogue — even in English, mapping it to Italian phrases.
  • 🎨 Learn her colours cold — look around the room right now and name 5 things in Italian. Do this daily.
  • 📏 Look up her Italian clothing size before lesson 5 — she'll need it in the role-plays.

Teacher Notes

  • Don't get into grammatical gender for adjectives (rosso/rossa, etc.) — it's too much at this stage. Just teach the base form and move on.
  • The role-play in section 5 is dense — if she's slower, trim the colour section (section 4) to 3 minutes and give the extra time to the role-play.
  • Lo prendo (I'll take it) is a great phrase to add at the end of the role-play — natural, short, and satisfying to say.
  • If she has energy left: teach È in saldo? (Is it on sale?) and Ha qualcosa di meno caro? (Do you have something cheaper?) — useful and memorable.
