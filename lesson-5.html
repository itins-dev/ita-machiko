Lesson 5 — Hotel Conversations

Check-in · Requests · Asking for Help · Beginner

⏱ 30 minutes 🎯 Beginner ✈️ Italy-bound
Lesson objective: By the end, she can check into a hotel, handle common requests at reception, ask for things she needs, and manage any problems — all without switching to English.
1

Warm-up — Full Series Review

Reconnect with everything she knows
3 min

Quick-fire flashcard drill across all four lessons: greetings, introductions, numbers, shopping phrases. Then set the scene: "It's 8pm. You've just arrived at your hotel in Rome after a long flight. What do you need to say?"

Let her think. This final lesson pulls everything together in the most practical context of the trip.

2

Checking In

Everything from arrival to getting the key
8 min
Italian English
Ho una prenotazioneI have a reservation
Il mio nome è…My name is…
Ho prenotato una cameraI booked a room
Una camera singola / doppiaA single / double room
Per quante notti?For how many nights? (what staff will ask)
Per [x] nottiFor [x] nights
A che ora è il check-out?What time is check-out?
Dov'è l'ascensore?Where is the lift?
Dov'è la mia camera?Where is my room?
Ho una prenotazione is the single most important phrase in this lesson — it unlocks the whole check-in interaction. Drill it until it's automatic.
3

Requests & Asking for Things

What to say when you need something
7 min
Italian English
Potrei avere…?Could I have…? (polite request)
Mi può portare…?Can you bring me…?
Asciugamani in piùExtra towels
Cuscini in piùExtra pillows
Il Wi-Fi non funzionaThe Wi-Fi isn't working
C'è un problema con la cameraThere's a problem with the room
L'aria condizionata non funzionaThe air conditioning isn't working
Può chiamarmi un taxi?Can you call me a taxi?
A che ora viene servita la colazione?What time is breakfast served?
Potrei avere…? is the most versatile request frame — more polite than vorrei in a hotel context. Teach her to slot anything into it: Potrei avere una bottiglia d'acqua?
Mini drill You play the receptionist. She calls down to request three different things — towels, a taxi, and the breakfast time. Keep it snappy: one phrase each.
4

Handling Problems

When things go wrong — staying calm and clear
5 min
Italian English Notes
Ho perso la chiave I've lost my key Most hotels use key cards now
La camera non è stata pulita The room hasn't been cleaned
C'è troppo rumore There's too much noise
Vorrei cambiare camera I'd like to change rooms
Posso avere la ricevuta? Can I have the receipt? At check-out
Faccio il check-out I'm checking out Simple, clear, works every time
Most hotel problems are solved with C'è un problema con… + the broken thing. She doesn't need to explain it fully — the staff will take over once they understand what's wrong.
5

Putting It Together — Full Hotel Stay

Check-in to check-out in one long role-play
5 min
Scenario She arrives at the hotel, checks in, discovers the Wi-Fi isn't working, calls reception for extra towels, asks what time breakfast is, then checks out the next morning and asks for the receipt.
Sample exchange
Reception (you): Buonasera! Benvenuta.
Student: Buonasera. Ho una prenotazione. Il mio nome è [name].
Reception: Sì, camera 214. Per quante notti?
Student: Per tre notti. A che ora è il check-out?
Reception: Alle undici. Ecco la sua chiave.
Student: Grazie. Dov'è l'ascensore?
— Later —
Student: Scusi — il Wi-Fi non funziona. E potrei avere degli asciugamani in più?
Reception: Certo, li mando subito.
— Next morning —
Student: Buongiorno. Faccio il check-out. Posso avere la ricevuta?
Reception: Eccola. Buon viaggio!
Student: Grazie mille!

Run it twice — first time slowly, second time at natural speed. This is the most complete role-play in the series: it draws on all five lessons at once.

6

Wrap-up & Final Homework

Consolidate the whole series before the trip
2 min

Final runthrough: mix phrases from all five lessons at random. She should be noticeably faster and more confident than lesson 1.

  • 🏨 Mentally rehearse the full check-in dialogue out loud — ideally the night before she travels. Repetition at night consolidates memory.
  • 📋 Write a personal cheat sheet: 2–3 key phrases from each lesson on a small card she can slip in her bag. It's a confidence prop — just having it reduces anxiety.
  • 🗣️ Commit to attempting Italian first in every interaction in Italy — even if she switches to English afterward. The attempt matters most.

Teacher Notes

  • This lesson lands hardest if it's taught close to her travel date — the relevance makes everything stick faster.
  • The full role-play in section 5 is the highlight of the series. Give it time. If the earlier sections run long, trim the problems section (4) to the top 3 phrases.
  • End by reminding her: she now has everything she needs. Greetings, introductions, numbers, shopping, hotels — that's the whole trip covered.
  • If she's keen to continue after Italy: suggest Lesson 6 could be Restaurants — Un tavolo per due, Il conto per favore — a natural next step.
